What actually happened under the hood:
The data wasn't manually cleaned in the traditional EDA sense — yfinance handles the heavy lifting. Here's the actual pipeline:

Collected — yf.download() hit Yahoo Finance's API and pulled adjusted closing prices for all 11 tickers + SPY in one call, returning a pandas DataFrame indexed by date
Structured — that DataFrame is essentially a dict of lists under the hood — each column is a ticker, each row is a trading day. Pandas just wraps it with labels
Filtered — .dropna() removed any days where prices were missing (holidays, non-trading days)
Computed — multiplied share counts × price each day to get portfolio value, then .pct_change() to get the daily return series
Sorted — the index is already time-ordered from yfinance, so no explicit sort needed
Plotted — matplotlib took those series directly and drew the lines

On benchmark collection:
SPY was pulled in the exact same yf.download() call as your tickers — just one extra string in the list. Same API, same date range, same format. Then we calculated how many SPY shares $66.63 would have bought on Aug 8 and tracked that forward.
